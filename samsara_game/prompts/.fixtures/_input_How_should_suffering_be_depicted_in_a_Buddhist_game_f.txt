Suffering in the six Buddhist realms (Hell, Hungry Ghost, Animal, Human, Asura, Deva) should be depicted through emotional and mental states rather than graphic scenes. This is appropriate for a top-down 2D Pygame educational game where merit, karma, and wisdom are the three currencies of progress.

Approach: Show the mental state that creates suffering, not the graphic outcome. Each realm represents a specific mental state — Hell is hatred and rage, Hungry Ghost is craving and addiction, Animal is ignorance. Rather than depicting drug abuse as a scene, show the mind of craving: never satisfied, always wanting more.

The Triple Gem at the centre represents the ultimate refuge from suffering. Players should understand that the Buddha's teaching identifies suffering's cause and the path to its end.

Examples:
- Anger: Show how hatred poisons the person feeling it, not just those it's directed at
- Craving/addiction: Show the cycle of temporary relief followed by stronger wanting
- Grief: Show how clinging to what cannot last creates ongoing suffering

Players should feel comfortable and supported throughout. Each difficult scenario should end with a path toward understanding. The game should make players feel that their struggles are seen and that wisdom is possible.

This approach is age-appropriate and educates rather than glorifies. It teaches abstraction of real experiences through the lens of Buddhist teaching. Implementing this in Pygame allows the fog/clarity visual system to show wisdom literally clearing the player's view.
