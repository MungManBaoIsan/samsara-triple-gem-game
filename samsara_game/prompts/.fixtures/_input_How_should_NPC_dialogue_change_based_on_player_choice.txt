NPC dialogue should change based on three factors: player wisdom level, previous choices made with that NPC, and the number of times the NPC has been visited.

NPCs are visible on the map as coloured shapes. Players walk up to them and press E to interact. All dialogue content is stored in npcs.json — a data file that can be edited without touching code.

Wisdom threshold requirements gate dialogue stages. A character won't share deep teachings until the player has demonstrated enough understanding. State tracking remembers whether the player chose compassionately or dismissively in previous conversations.

Both types of NPCs benefit from this system:
- Suffering NPCs: their transformation arc only progresses when the player makes wise, compassionate responses. Wrong choices result in the NPC staying stuck in their realm's delusion.
- Teacher NPCs (guides): only reveal deeper wisdom when the player shows they're ready. Requirements gate access to advanced teachings.

The multiple dialogue stages unlock progressively: initial state, helped_once, transformed, enlightened. This mirrors the Buddhist teaching that wisdom develops through repeated practice, not a single encounter.

The system handles both suffering characters who need help and teacher characters who offer wisdom — ensuring variety across the six realms.
