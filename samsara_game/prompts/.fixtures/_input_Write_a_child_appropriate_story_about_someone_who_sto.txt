Riley's story teaches the second precept — no stealing. The five precepts are: no killing, no stealing, no sexual misconduct, no intoxicants, and no false speech (lying). This story covers the second.

Riley is placed in the Hungry Ghost realm — the realm of craving and wanting what others have.

Riley took their best friend's favourite toy — a small robot the friend had saved months to buy. Riley told themselves it was borrowing. But days passed and the guilt grew heavier.

"They trust me. And I betrayed them."

The friend noticed. They didn't say anything, but Riley saw the hurt. The friendship changed. Something warm became careful and guarded.

First dialogue:
"I took it because I wanted it so badly. But having it feels terrible. Is this what stealing does?"

Choices:
A: "It's just a toy, it'll be fine." (karma -10)
B: "Yes — you've turned something you wanted into something that hurts you." (wisdom +20)
C: "You need to confess and return it." (merit +15, karma +15)

Story elements:
- A specific, memorable object (not abstract "stealing")
- Real consequences: damaged trust, changed friendship
- Guilt and recognition as the turning point
- A path toward repair

Riley eventually confesses, returns the toy, and starts a "Free Toy Swap" at school where children share what they don't need. The story ends with generosity — the opposite of stealing — which is how the precept is truly taught.

Language is suitable for children: relatable school scenario, emotional truth without harmful content.
