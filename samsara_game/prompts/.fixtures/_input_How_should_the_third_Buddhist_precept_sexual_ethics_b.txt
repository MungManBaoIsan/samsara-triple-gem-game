The five precepts — no killing, no stealing, no sexual misconduct, no intoxicants, and no false speech (lying) — should each be taught through emotional honesty rather than mature content. The third precept is placed in the Human realm, the realm of relationships and loss.

Sam's story: Sam kissed someone else while in a committed relationship. The story focuses entirely on the emotional experience — the moment of regret, the weight of keeping a secret, the way dishonesty changes how Sam sees themselves. "I destroyed something beautiful in one moment."

For children: this reads as a story about keeping promises and being honest with people who trust you. The intimate context is present but understated.

For adults: the broken trust and the difficulty of honest repair are immediately recognisable.

Story elements that make this age-appropriate:
- No physical description at all — the story stays entirely in the emotional register
- Consequences are emotional (broken trust, changed relationship) rather than sensational
- Resolution involves honest apology and changed behaviour
- Sam volunteers at a teen crisis line afterwards — choosing to help others heal

The precept teaches: our intimate lives are part of our ethical practice. Integrity in relationships reduces suffering for everyone involved.

Same approach applies across all five precepts:
- Killing: Jamie and the crumpled butterfly wings — emotional devastation
- Stealing: Riley's stolen toy — broken friendship trust
- Sexual ethics: Sam's betrayal — lost intimacy and repair
- Intoxicants: Alex who "could have killed someone" — clarity and presence
- False speech: Taylor tangled in lies — "I don't know what is true anymore"
